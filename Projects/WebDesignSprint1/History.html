History of Generative AI


    • 1950s: HMM (hidden markov models) and GMMs (Gaussian Mixture Models) were the first generative models; they produced successive pieces of data nbsp;such as speech

    • 1960s: ELIZA was a chat box created by Joseph Weizenbaum, it is one if the first examples of Natural Language Processing (NLP) and could communicate with humans in natual language

    • 1980s: Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are used for language modeling tasks. They can model relatively long dependencies and allow generating longer sentences

    • 2014: Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm that engages 2 neural networks that are in competition with each other. One network is a generative model that generates content, while the other is discriminative that tries to figure out whether it is an authentic sample or not.

    • 2017: Transformer architecture models are intended to process sequences Transformer architecture models are intended to process sequences language models such as Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers (BERT) and Generative Pre-TrainingTransformer (GPT)

The Founding Fathers of AI


Alan Turing

Turing developed a test in 1950 that could evaluate if a computer is intelligent enough to pass as a human being. The Turing Test , involves a computer, a human interrogator, and a human interviewee, and The conversations are made via a keyboard and a display screen. Only in 2014 was a computer able to pass the Turing test.


Allen Newell & Herbert A. Simon

Newell's goal was to make the computer an effective tool for stimulating human pronlem solving. Simon developed a research career on the nature of intelligence, with a focus in problem-solving and decision making. The two started working together in the 1950s and founded the first hub dedicated to studying AI at Carnegie Mellon University.


John McCarthy

In 1964 McCarthy became the founding director of the Standford Artificial Intelligence Labratory. This research center was one of the most prominent places for the development of technology during the 1960s and 1970s and played a crucial part in machine-vision natural language and robotics.


Marvin Minksy

His passion and goal to endow machines with the intelligence eventually drove him to be the first electronic learning systems creator. The project would become the original neural network stimulator.
